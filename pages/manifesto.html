Dear Future Me, Somewhere Out There...

I’m not someone who has traveled far and wide yet. When I think about going somewhere new, I worry I won’t know what to do. I don’t want to leave a place feeling regretful, like I missed something meaningful or forgot to document what it made me feel. So I’ve created this little checklist, a personal travel manifesto, to keep me grounded and intentional. These are some key aspects I want to explore should I ever get the chance to travel. My hope is to leave each place with no regrets, only memories.

My Travel Manifesto

  • I will research local cultures and customs before I go, and remain respectful to them while I’m there.
  • I will try local food with curiosity, using it as a way to connect with the place and its people.
  • I will jot down my feelings and emotional reactions in real time while I’m still in the place, not just after.
  • I will learn the history of the places I visit, not just take pictures of the beautiful parts.
  • I will collect one meaningful token or keepsake from every place I visit as a way to remember it deeply.
  • I will capture my journey through art and poetry whether it’s a haiku, a sketch, or a metaphor—so the emotional landscape doesn’t fade over time.

This manifesto isn’t about doing travel “right”;it’s about being honest, present, and intentional. I want to travel in a way that honors where I am and who I’m becoming. Wherever I go, may I stay open, grounded, and grateful.

With hope,
Me
